Gangstar: New York is part of the Gangstar series, which is
known for its open-world gameplay inspired by the Grand
Theft Auto franchise. Similar to other games in the series,
Gangstar: New York features a mix of story-driven missions,
as well as opportunities for free-roaming and causing chaos
in the city.
In Gangstar: New York, developed in Unreal Engine 4.27, players take on the
role of a gangster navigating the criminal underworld of New York City. The
game offers a vast open-world environment where players can explore the
city, engage in various missions, and side activities, and interact with NPCs.
Contributions:
• Wrote the design documentation, and worked closely with the programing and UI/UX
departments on iterating and implementing the system which facilitated the player interaction
and navigation with a controller/gamepad;
• I designed and wrote the documentation for the controller Aim Assist feature. Having experience
playing games like Halo, GTA and Battlefield on consoles I was given this task, and together with
the programming department, through constant playtests, while tweaking and iterating of the
game’s advanced camera and controls systems, we managed to deliver an Aim Assist feature, that
was on par with the before mentioned games;
• Created the documentation of the Weapon Mastery System, a feature meant to award the player
with medals and badges that also would also an experience points bonus upon completing the
requirements, and allowed the player to equip them, and have them displayed for everyone to
see during gameplay (ex: “Perform X Consecutive headshots with X SMG”)
• Worked on the game and level design of the Free For All multiplayer game mode by: placing and
configuring the game mode trigger, isolating and adding boundaries to the FFA play area, placing
cover areas and player spawn points and setting the rules of the game mode: global time, time to
respawn, limited invulnerability after respawn, allowed weapons, damage over time when outside
play area;
• I assisted the Nintendo Switch team, with various tasks from a design standpoint, that were aimed
at improving game performance and reduce memory usage by: checking and mapping the entire
game level, identifying the objects and effects that were too heavy to be used on the Switch
console, and either excluding them from the build, or working with the artists that created them,
and finding solutions that would optimize them for better performance.

PERSONAL
PROJECTS
Fay is a 3 Chapter 3rd Person Fantasy
Action RPG created in Unreal Engine 5
and C++, that me as a Game Designer
and Andreea Popescu (see CV here) as an
enthusiast programmer, have started
working on since September 2023 – see
DevLog here.
The main protagonist, Fay is a fairy that fell from
the Fairy World, resulting in her losing her
memories and forgetting how to fly and use her
powers.
Throughout the game, the player will embark
into Fay’s journey and learn different abilities
such as melee and ranged combat, climbing, and
ultimately flying, all by finding Scrolls and
completing Quests.
FAY
What we have so far:
- A fully rigged detailed main character (Fay) that can jump, roll,
sprint, and attack using melee weapons;
- Wall climbing system that allows the MC to escalate vertical walls
and navigate obstacles;
- Melee weapons with different attack patterns and attack
animations;
- A UI system that keeps track of the MC’s HP and Stamina, the
enemies HP and damage that is being applied to them;
- Enemies that have a patrol system where they roam around and
engage and attack when the player gets in their aggro range.
What we are working on:
- The game level of the 1
st
Chapter, that will be
comprised of: forest-like environment with diverse
terrains, village, farm fields, shrine and maze-like caves
leading to the Final Boss;
- Scripting the Boss type enemies with special abilities
(AoE and DoT type spells, spawning henchmen, etc.);
- Implementing unique setups for the boss encounters
and mini-bosses;
- An inventory system in order to manage the game’s
items: weapons, potions, scrolls and other items that
can be collected and used within the game;
- Progression system that is tied to completing quests
and finding Scrolls, that would result in the acquisition of
new abilities and equipment throughout the game;
- Varied sound and particle effects in order to enhance
immersion and highlight environmental cues.
